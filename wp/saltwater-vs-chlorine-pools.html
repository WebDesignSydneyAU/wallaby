Saltwater vs chlorine pools

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the main difference between a saltwater and chlorine pool?
The main difference lies in how they are sanitized. Chlorine pools use chlorine tablets, while saltwater pools have a generator that uses salt to produce natural chlorine.
Is maintaining a saltwater pool more or less expensive than maintaining a traditional chlorine pool?
While the upfront cost of installing a saltwater system can be higher, the ongoing maintenance costs tend to be lower because you dont need to buy as much chemicals like in traditional chlorine pools.
Which type of pool is safer for skin and eyes, saltwater or chlorine?
Saltwater pools are generally gentler on skin and eyes compared to traditional chlorine pools. They have lower levels of chloramines which cause irritation.
How often does a saltwater pool need to be serviced compared to a traditional chlorine pool?
Both types of pools require regular maintenance, but traditional chlorine pools typically require more frequent servicing due to the need for constant addition of chemicals.
Can I convert my existing chlorine swimming pool into a saltwater swimming pool?
Yes, its possible to convert your existing traditional chlorinated swimming pool into a salt water system. This process usually involves installing a new pump and filtration system designed specifically for use with saline water.
